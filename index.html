Coop Blog 2

Introduction

For the second half of my 8-month co-op as a Student Consultant at the University of Guelph, I embraced more challenging responsibilities and had the opportunity to mentor new co-op students. This allowed me to develop leadership skills and tackle complex problems, improving my technical expertise and adaptability. Through these experiences, I deepened my understanding of IT systems and strengthened my ability to collaborate effectively with the team. This also gave me valuable insights into the importance of communication and teamwork in IT support.

Information about the Employer

At the University of Guelph, CCS (Computing and Communications Services) is the heart of all technology on campus. From managing Wi-Fi access to ensuring Gryph Mail and Office 365 run smoothly, CCS is dedicated to providing top-notch IT support for the entire campus community. With their office conveniently located on campus, they are always ready to solve any tech-related issues for students, faculty, and staff.

Coop Goals

For my co-op term, my goals and learning outcomes included:


  • Enhancing Conflict Resolution Skills

    Goal & Action Plan : Improve conflict resolution skills by attending conflict management training and applying these skills in client interactions. Focus on staying calm, listening actively, and offering solutions to resolve issues professionally.
    Measure of Success : Reduction in escalated issues and positive feedback from clients and supervisors on handling difficult situations.
    Benefit for Future Work terms : Enhanced ability to manage client relationships, stay composed under pressure, and contribute to effective teamwork in challenging situations.


  • Developing Leadership Skills

    Goal & Action Plan : Develop leadership skills by volunteering to lead team meetings, assisting newer team members, and taking initiative in activities.
    Measure of Success : Positive peer and supervisor feedback on leadership contributions and overall effectiveness in supporting the team.
    Benefit for Future Work terms : Strengthened leadership abilities will enable me to guide teams more effectively, take on higher responsibilities, and contribute to team success in future roles.


  • Deepening Understanding of Networking

    Goal & Action Plan : Deepen my understanding of networking principles by studying networking concepts, shadowing networking experts at CCS, and troubleshooting network-related issues at the Help Desk.
    Measure of Success : Ability to independently resolve network-related issues, with positive feedback from team members and a reduction in escalations for networking problems.
    Benefit for Future Work terms : Improved networking knowledge will enable me to support clients more effectively, troubleshoot issues efficiently, and contribute to solving complex technical problems in future roles.


  • Improving Time Management

    Goal & Action Plan : Improve time management by prioritizing tasks with to-do lists, time blocks, and project management tools to meet deadlines.
    Measure of Success : Consistently meeting deadlines and receiving positive feedback on work quality and timeliness.
    Benefit for Future Work terms : Better time management will allow me to handle multiple tasks efficiently and meet deadlines while maintaining work quality.

Conclusion

In this blog, I have shared my co-op experience at the University of Guelph, focusing on the development of key skills like conflict resolution, leadership, networking, and time management. These experiences have strengthened my ability to handle challenges and contribute effectively to the team, preparing me for future work terms and my career ahead.
